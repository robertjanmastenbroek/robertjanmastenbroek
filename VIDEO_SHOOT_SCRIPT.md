# SEND ME — 60 sec video · line-by-line shoot script

*Shoot each line as its own take. Edit together later. Don't memorise — read once, look just past the lens like you're talking to one person you love, then shoot.*

---

## THE B-ROLL DECISION — read this first

**You asked: should I do all the B-roll, or just talk to camera?**

**Talking head. All 11 lines. One exception.**

Three reasons this is the right call:

1. **Authenticity wins the ask.** The video is a confession, not a commercial. Confessions happen on a face, not in a montage. Your face, holding the eye contact, is the whole argument. The moment you cut away to staged B-roll, the ask starts feeling rehearsed — and "rehearsed" reads as "paid-for" the instant money is on the table.

2. **You sold the van.** You no longer own the thing a van-montage would need. Any van footage would be stock, borrowed, or a rental — and the audience will smell it. The absence of B-roll literally IS the scarcity that makes the ask true.

3. **One asset, not ten.** A polished multi-location shoot adds weeks of scheduling and turns this into a project. Talking head can be shot in one afternoon, one phone, one wall, one window. Ship this week — not next month.

**The one exception:** Line 6 ("140 BPM. Tribal psytrance. People scream IN THE NAME OF JESUS…"). For 6–7 seconds here, cut to *existing* Holy Rave crowd footage you already own. 2–3 fast cuts. This is the only moment where the room speaks louder than the face. Return to your face at "don't know why."

**What you do NOT need to shoot:** no beach, no road, no horizon, no diesel tank, no speakers being loaded, no sunrise. Zero new B-roll.

**What you DO shoot:** yourself, 11 times, against one simple background. That is the whole production.

---

## BEFORE YOU HIT RECORD

- **Camera:** phone, vertical (9:16), 4K if available.
- **Lighting:** natural daylight. Face a window, not the sun. Shoot between 10:00–12:00 or 16:00–18:00.
- **Background:** one simple wall. White, cream, or textured stone. Nothing behind you that moves or reads as "lifestyle." The background is silence — the face does the talking.
- **Audio:** second phone in your chest pocket on voice memo. Clap once before each take so you can sync in post. Camera-only audio is the fallback.
- **Wardrobe:** what you always wear. Don't costume-up.
- **Eye contact:** lens, not yourself on the screen. Tape over the preview if you cheat.
- **Pacing:** read the line once in your head, breathe, then say it like you're telling a friend the first time.

Shoot each line **three times.** Best take wins. Don't settle for the first.

## THE LINES — shoot in any order

### LINE 1 — 3 seconds
> **"For almost six years, I lived in a campervan."**

*Delivery:* calm, matter-of-fact, almost shy. Not dramatic.
*Setting:* direct to camera, close-up. Your face is the shot.

---

### LINE 2 — 4 seconds
> **"I was a millionaire at twenty-seven. Lost everything at thirty. In the papers. In public."**

*Delivery:* steady. Not bitter, not proud. Just fact.
*Setting:* same close-up. Direct to camera.

---

### LINE 3 — 3 seconds
> **"I ended up busking streets in Tenerife."**

*Delivery:* quiet. Not self-pitying. You're describing a scene, not complaining.
*Setting:* direct to camera.

---

### LINE 4 — 5 seconds
> **"One night, on my knees inside the van, I asked Jesus to make me happy like I was as a child."**

*Delivery:* the most important line of the first half. Slow. Soft. Don't perform.
*Setting:* direct to camera. Don't break eye contact. Let "as a child" land before you cut.

---

### LINE 5 — 2 seconds (THE HINGE)
> **"He did."**

*Delivery:* tiny smile. Two-word sentence. Eye contact. Let it breathe.
*Setting:* close-up. No music yet. This is the quietest moment. Music lifts immediately after.

---

### LINE 6 — 3 seconds
> **"I was baptized on a beach in Tenerife not long after."**

*Delivery:* plain. Don't explain it, don't underline it. Just say it the way you'd say "I got a haircut."
*Setting:* direct to camera.

---

### LINE 7 — 5 seconds
> **"Last summer I sold the van. I thought I was meant to settle. I was wrong."**

*Delivery:* brief pause between the three sentences. "I was wrong" lands with a small head-shake, no smile.
*Setting:* direct to camera. This is the moment the ask becomes necessary — let the audience feel why.

---

### LINE 8 — 7 seconds *(ONLY B-ROLL SECTION)*
> **"Now I make music. 140 BPM. Tribal psytrance. People scream IN THE NAME OF JESUS at a rave and don't know why."**

*Delivery:* energy lifts. Faster pace. Half-laugh on the last clause.
*Setting:* voiceover over existing Holy Rave crowd footage. 2–3 fast cuts. Return to your face on "don't know why."

---

### LINE 9 — 7 seconds
> **"I play preferably for free. Because the people who might need this most are usually the ones who can't afford a ticket."**

*Delivery:* steady. This is your thesis. Say it like you mean it.
*Setting:* direct to camera. Eye contact on "preferably for free."

---

### LINE 10 — 7 seconds
> **"I want to bring this everywhere. A new camper. A rig. A tank of diesel. Wherever I'm sent."**

*Delivery:* list cadence. Tiny pause between "new camper", "rig", "tank of diesel". Let "wherever I'm sent" land.
*Setting:* direct to camera. No B-roll. The absence of the van is the point.

---

### LINE 11 — 8 seconds
> **"If Holy Rave has ever moved something in you — this is how you keep the door open for the next person walking in."**

*Delivery:* direct, personal. This is the ask. Eye contact lens.
*Setting:* direct to camera. Your face. Nothing else.

---

### LINE 12 — 4 seconds
> **"Any amount. Monthly or once. It all goes to the road."**

*Delivery:* short sentences. Slight pause between each.
*Setting:* direct to camera.

---

### LINE 13 — 5 seconds (CLOSE)
> **"Here am I. Send me."**

*Delivery:* the quietest, most confident line of all thirteen. No smile, then a tiny smile, then cut.
*Setting:* direct to camera. Close-up. Hold on your face for 1 full second after you stop talking. Then cut to black.

---

## TOTAL: ~60 seconds of spoken audio

Add 1 second of breathing room at open and close = 61 seconds raw. Edit will land at 58–60 seconds.
