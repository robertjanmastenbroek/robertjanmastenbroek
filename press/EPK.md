## The Full Story

Robert-Jan Mastenbroek has walked away from a record deal, a million-dollar business, and a spiritual school with 1,200 students — each time for the same reason: it wasn't how it was supposed to be.

At five years old, he was recording songs off the radio onto a cassette player. His first CD was Queen's Greatest Hits — played until it wore out. He made a guitar from a tennis racket with "Red Special" written on A4 paper. From the beginning, it was never about performing. It was about *being* music.

He built Dream or Donate, the largest Dutch/Belgian crowdfunding platform — €6 million raised. Walked away from a major record deal at 21 because it meant becoming "a monkey on a stage." Became a millionaire through coaching, crypto, real estate. Attended a Tony Robbins event with five-thousand-dollar front-row seats, watched the manufactured emotion, and understood: all of this is people paying to be pumped up. It left him empty.

Then everything collapsed. The platform was hacked. He was blackmailed. He gave away his Bitcoin to people who broke their promises. The relationship ended. Six years in a campervan — across Europe and Tenerife — building himself back from nothing. Started busking in the streets: 10€/hour, then 100€/hour, then restaurants, bars, weddings, five-star hotels, 750€/hour for private business events. Peak earning. Still not happy.

He got on his knees on a beach in Tenerife and asked Jesus to make him happy again — like he was as a child, before the money, the status, the noise.

What emerged was **Holy Rave**.

> *"I feel happy as a child all day, every day now. Because I know I have a father in heaven who loves me. When I walk. When I'm drinking coffee. When I'm doing my shows. A lot. A lot."*

He now plays wherever he can. Preferably free. Because the people who might need this most are usually the ones who can't afford a ticket.

## Holy Rave — The Live Show

Wherever Robert-Jan plays, the rave becomes holy.

His sets combine 130–140 BPM tribal psytrance and melodic techno with live vocals, Hebrew prayer mantras, and biblical lyrics. Between tracks: a short prayer — never a sermon, always specific, always under 30 seconds.

*"Thank you God for these bodies we can dance with. For the sunshine, the ocean, the voices we can sing with. In the name of Jesus — let's go."*

Then the next track drops.

People who've never set foot in a church stand on a dancefloor and feel something they can't name. Something that stays after the night ends. They tell their friends the next day: *"Man — you just should have been there."*

## For Podcasts & Interviews

Robert-Jan is an open book. His story — the millionaire, the campervan, the busking, the beach baptism — is one of the more unusual origin stories in electronic music. Subjects he speaks to well:
