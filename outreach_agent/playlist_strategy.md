# RJM Own Spotify Playlist Strategy
### Build followable playlists that funnel strangers to Robert-Jan Mastenbroek tracks

---

## Why This Works

Spotify's discovery algorithm surfaces playlists in search. When someone searches "tribal techno 2026" and finds a great playlist with 20+ tracks, they follow it — then RJM's tracks at positions 7–10 get streamed. Featured artists reshare the playlist (free promo). Followers get notified on future updates.

**Target:** 500 followers per playlist within 60 days of creation. 1,000 within 90 days.

## Execution Plan

### Week 1 — Create all 5 playlists
1. Create each playlist on Spotify with the exact titles above (keywords drive search discovery)
2. Paste the exact descriptions above into each playlist description field
3. Add all 20 tracks (verify each track exists on Spotify before adding — use Spotify search)
4. Make all playlists **public** (not private, not collaborative)
5. Set cover art: atmospheric 1:1 photo, no text, no logo — pure aesthetic
   - Playlist 1: dark tribal/desert imagery
   - Playlist 2: geometric/mandala or fractal
   - Playlist 3: light, devotional, candlelight
   - Playlist 4: Tenerife ocean at golden hour
   - Playlist 5: person working alone, moody lighting

### Week 2–4 — Outreach to featured artists
For each playlist, identify Instagram handles or email for 5 featured artists and send:
> *"Hey [Artist] — I curated a [genre] playlist and included your track [Track Name]. Would love it if you gave it a share — here's the link: [URL]. Keep making great music."*
- No self-promotion in the message — let the playlist earn the share
- Result: established artists share the playlist to their audiences → new followers discover RJM

### Ongoing — Monthly updates
- Add 2–3 new tracks per month (Spotify notifies followers)
- Swap out lower-performing tracks based on playlist listener data
- Always maintain RJM tracks in positions 5–10 (not positions 1–3 — build credibility first)
