## How to Write a SubmitHub Pitch

SubmitHub pitches are NOT cold emails. They are short, track-focused, no-fluff submissions. Curators read hundreds per week.

**Rules:**
- Maximum 100 words — curators skip anything longer
- Start with the track, not your biography
- One specific reason why this fits THEIR playlist
- End with one unique fact about the artist (not a list of achievements)
- No links except the track (SubmitHub embeds the player automatically)

**What NOT to write:**
- "I've been making music for X years..."
- "I hope you enjoy this track as much as I enjoyed making it..."
- "Please consider this for your incredible playlist..."
- Stream counts, follower counts, or any vanity metrics (unless exceptional)

## RJM Template Pitch — SubmitHub (98 words)

```
[TRACK NAME] is a [BPM] BPM [genre] track built on [key element — e.g. Hebrew 
vocal chanting / tribal percussion / dark psytrance production].

It fits your playlist because [one specific reason: e.g. "the tribal percussion 
and 140 BPM structure sits directly beside your existing Vini Vici and 
Hilight Tribe selections"].

Robert-Jan Mastenbroek is a Dutch producer based in Tenerife making 
Bible-inspired tribal techno — ancient scripture carried by future sound. 
He rebuilt his music career after losing a €6M company. 

This track is his most [accessible / hypnotic / dark] work to date.
```

**Customise per curator:**
- Replace [TRACK NAME] with the specific track most relevant to their genre
- Replace the genre and BPM with correct values
- In "fits your playlist because..." — name 1–2 actual tracks from their playlist that RJM sits near
- This single customisation lifts approval rate significantly
