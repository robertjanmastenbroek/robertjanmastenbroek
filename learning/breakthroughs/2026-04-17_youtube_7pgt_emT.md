**What Made This Work:**

• **Tension hook + constant B-roll motion prevented scroll decay** — The 44.3% completion rate (2x baseline) came from tension creating narrative pull, while continuous B-roll eliminated static frames that trigger mid-video bounces. No friction to exit.

• **14-second duration locked the completion window** — Long enough to resolve the tension payoff, short enough that viewers' attention span doesn't fracture. Optimal for YouTube's short-form algorithm weighting (completion % is the strongest ranking signal).

• **Visual storytelling > text-dependent hook** — B-roll works because it doesn't require captions, sound-on viewing, or cognitive load. Viewers can follow the tension arc purely through pacing and imagery, reducing friction for silent scrollers.

• **The save/scroll-stop paradox reveals the weakness** — 0% saves + 0% scroll-stop means this drives *passive completion*, not active engagement. Viewers finished but didn't find it bookmark-worthy or click on anything. This is funnel content, not viral content.
