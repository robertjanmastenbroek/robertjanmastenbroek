## Photography & Artwork Direction

### Portrait Style
**Reference images showed clear approach:**

1. **Centered composition** (subject directly facing camera)
2. **High contrast lighting** (dramatic shadows or pure white backdrops)
3. **Minimal background** (remove distractions, focus on subject)
4. **Symmetrical framing** (balanced, authoritative presence)
5. **Direct eye contact or obscured face** (connection or mystery, not in-between)

### Color Grading Options

**Option A: Ancient/Warm**
- Desaturated earth tones
- Warm highlights (golden hour feel)
- Textured, tactile, weathered
- Film grain for timeless quality

**Option B: Futuristic/Cool**
- High-key white or low-key black
- Cool blue accents
- Crisp, ultra-sharp focus
- Clean, almost clinical perfection

**Option C: Hybrid (Recommended)**
- Start with ancient/warm base
- Add futuristic/cool accents (glowing eyes, chrome elements)
- Blend both worlds in single image

## Thumbnail & Cover Art Rules

Based on your aesthetic and YouTube optimization:

### Composition
1. **Center the subject** - Face or key element dead-center
2. **High contrast** - Dark vs. light, ancient vs. future
3. **Sacred geometry** - Triangles, diamonds framing the subject
4. **Minimal text** - Your name + "Ancient Truth. Future Sound." only
5. **Breathing room** - Don't crowd edges (mobile crop consideration)

### Typography
- **Primary:** Bold, geometric, uppercase (e.g., "ROBERT-JAN MASTENBROEK")
- **Secondary:** Clean sans-serif, lighter weight (e.g., "Ancient Truth. Future Sound.")
- **Avoid:** Decorative fonts, script, anything "churchy"

### Color Blocking
- Dark background + light subject (or inverse)
- ONE accent color max per image (gold or blue, not both)
- 70/30 rule: 70% one tone, 30% contrast

## Do's and Don'ts

### ‚úÖ DO
- Mix ancient textures with futuristic forms
- Use high contrast (dark/light, old/new)
- Center your compositions
- Keep backgrounds minimal
- Use symmetry and sacred geometry
- Show craftsmanship and detail
- Maintain mystery (hooded figures, obscured faces)
- Use metallic accents (gold, bronze, silver, chrome)

### ‚ùå DON'T
- Use obvious Christian imagery (crosses, doves, church buildings)
- Add cheesy light rays or halos
- Use overly decorative or "worship" fonts
- Include text-heavy designs
- Crowd the composition
- Mix too many colors (keep it restrained)
- Use stock "DJ at turntables" photos (overused, generic)
- Copy other artists' exact style (find your unique blend)
