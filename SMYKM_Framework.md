# SMYKM Email Outreach Framework
*Applied to all podcast pitch emails from Robert-Jan Mastenbroek*

---

## The 7-Step Architecture (never skip, never reorder)

1. **Subject Line** — 2–4 words. Lowercase/sentence case. Hyper-specific to this person. If it applies to anyone else, rewrite it.
2. **SMYKM Trigger** — Open with a specific researched observation: a recent episode quote, a LinkedIn post, a stated belief, a career shift. NO pleasantries.
3. **Transition** — Seamless bridge from the observation to why RJ belongs on the show.
4. **Challenge** — Name the specific pain this host likely has (not industry-generic — this host, this show).
5. **Value Proposition** — RJ's specific story angle mapped directly to the challenge.
6. **Hidden Objection** — Defuse the most likely reason they'll ignore this in one sentence.
7. **Close** — One low-friction interest question. No meeting asks. No calendar links.

---

## Core Copywriting Filters

- **Visualization Rule** — Concrete imagery over abstract concepts
- **Falsifiability Rule** — Objective, provable facts only. No "great", "best", "unique"
- **Uniqueness Test** — Could a competitor sign their name to this claim? If yes, delete it
- **"One Mississippi" Test** — Core value prop must land in under 2 seconds
- **Point A → B** — Take them from current state (frustration, apathy) to desired state (relief, curiosity)
- **Metonymy** — Physical objects to represent complex concepts

---

## Hard Rules

- **Max 120 words** (entire body, excluding "NOTE TO RJ" header on fallback drafts)
- **Paragraphs**: 1–2 sentences max. White space is copy.
- **Tone**: Confident, peer-to-peer, modern. Never preachy.
- **No Christianese / faith jargon** — meaning is discovered, not announced
- **Language**: English (EN shows), Dutch (NL/BE shows), German (DE/AT/CH shows) — no others

### Banned Phrases
- "I am reaching out because..."
- "We help [X] do [Y]..."
- "Just following up..."
- "I know you are busy..."
- "I hope this finds you well..."
- "I wanted to reach out..."
- "My name is Robert-Jan and I..."

## Adaptation for Podcast Guest Pitching

The SMYKM "challenge" for hosts = **finding guests with genuinely rare stories** (not just "successful entrepreneurs").
