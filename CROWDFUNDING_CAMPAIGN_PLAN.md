## 2 · CAMPAIGN NAME — "SEND ME"

> *"Then I heard the voice of the Lord saying, 'Whom shall I send, and who will go for us?' And I said: Here am I. Send me."* — **Isaiah 6:8**

### Why this is the anchor
- **Subtle-salt perfect.** Seekers who know the verse will feel it. Seculars read "send me" as an imperative the *audience* gives the artist — a beautiful double meaning. *They* send *you*. *God* also sends you. Both are true in the same sentence.
- **Visualizable.** You see a man standing up. You see him going.
- **Unique.** No competitor can sign their name to this verse in this context.
- **Falsifiable.** "Sent" is provable — the receipts are the miles, the cities, the free nights.
- **One-Mississippi.** Two words.

### Usage
- Primary scripture on the offering page (replaces Psalm 150:6 at top of page; Psalm 150:6 stays elsewhere on the site).

- Hashtag: **#sendmetour** — new, ownable, googleable, tweetable.

## 3 · THE GOAL ARCHITECTURE

Three goals, three levels of visibility.

| Goal | Amount | Visibility | Displayed as |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Monthly floor** | €3,000 / mo MRR | **Tracked internally, never displayed** | No counter. Instead: the *story* of what each tier unlocks. |
| **The Road** (camper + rig) | €30,000 one-time | **Referenced visually but not priced** | A single line on the page + in the video: *"A camper and a rig. So I can drive to the people who need this most."* No number. No thermometer. |
| **Recurring supporters** | (count, not euros) | *Optional* tasteful display | *"247 people keeping this free."* Never euros. Only people. |

### Why no counter
Three reasons, in order of weight:

1. **It breaks the subtle-salt principle.** A counter is performative — it stages the ask. Performative holiness is the villain.
2. **It anchors giving downward.** If it says €427, people feel their €15 won't move it. If it says €4,000, they feel *you don't need them.* Either way it suppresses giving. This is documented in crowdfunding psychology — progress bars work for product launches, not for mission support.
3. **It turns the dancefloor into a scoreboard.** Holy Rave is free specifically because it is *not* a transaction. A counter silently contradicts that.

### Optional tasteful replacement (if you want *any* social proof)
A single, quiet line at the bottom of the tier section — *"247 people keeping Holy Rave free this month."* Humans, not euros. Tribal count, not scoreboard. I'd ship without it at launch and add it later only if conversion needs it.

## 9 · THE CAMPER VAN QUESTION — how to handle it

You told me three things about the van:
1. You want one.
2. ~€30K ballpark.
3. The *reason* is so you can bring Holy Rave free to the places no one else goes.

**Recommendation: don't make it a line item. Make it a scene.**

### Why not a line item / thermometer

- A "€30,000 to buy RJM a camper" counter reads as *personal* spending, not mission spending. The line between mission and personal gets blurry fast, and donors feel it.
- It caps the ceiling. Once you hit €30K, people stop giving. You *never* want a ceiling.
- It undermines the monthly recurring ask. People pick "the one-time van gift" over the €15/mo because it feels more fun. You want MRR, not spikes.

### Instead — treat the van as an aesthetic centerpiece

- **Photograph it.** When you get it, document the build. The dust. The speakers going in. The first sunrise out of the back doors. This becomes a year of content.
- **Name it.** A van with a name is a character. ("Jericho" would be the obvious choice.)
- **Thank donors by showing them where the van went.** Every city you roll into is a thank-you post: *"This week the van rolled into [x]. Free night. 200 people. Thank you to everyone keeping the door open."*

### When to reveal the van publicly

- **Not until you buy it.** Don't promise. Deliver.
- The day you have the keys: post a single photo, one line: *"The road just got longer. Thank you."*
- Then it becomes part of the ongoing story, not a fundraising line item.

### If a donor offers to fund the van directly

Some rich person WILL offer this in your inner circle once the campaign goes out. Say yes. Let them have that entire story. It will be the single most moving piece of content of the year. Just make sure they know it's not anonymous-by-default — ask if they want a public thank-you (some will; some won't).

## 13 · FAILURE MODES — what could go wrong

1. **Counter absence feels weird to some people** → mitigation: the "What your support does" three-card section replaces the visual anchor. You're giving the eye something to land on.
2. **Video feels like a pitch** → mitigation: the script opens with story, not ask. Ask is at 0:42, fifteen seconds before the end. Classic rule: earn the ask with the story.
3. **€100 minimum on tier 4 blocks someone who wants to give €50** → mitigation: they can give €33 (Patron) or two subscriptions of €15. Don't lower the minimum; it's an anchor.

5. **The van becomes the story instead of the mission** → mitigation: §9 framing holds. Van is a scene, not a line item.
6. **Someone writes a "RJM asking for money" hit piece in Dutch press** → mitigation: pre-empt with total transparency when asked. The campaign is visible, public, and the narrative is Holy Rave free — not self-enrichment. Your existing `/story` page is the pre-emptive answer: you've been through this cycle before, and this time you're not hiding the finances.
