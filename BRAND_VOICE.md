# BRAND VOICE — Robert-Jan Mastenbroek / Holy Rave

## PART 1 — THE FOUNDATION

### The Mission (20-year sentence — his own words)
> "It shows that Jesus is everywhere, even in places where you thought He wouldn't be present, like raves."

### The WHY (Sinek format)
To prove that Jesus is everywhere — even where you think He isn't — so that the people searching in all the wrong places can finally find what they were always looking for.

### The Compass (The Only Filter That Matters)
Before every decision — content, booking, partnership, collaboration, business deal — ask one question:
**"Is this how God intended it?"**
If yes: proceed.
If doubt: wait.
If no: walk away.

This is the same test that ended the record deal at 21, the spiritual school with 1,200 students, and the institutional church chapter. It is the only test that has never been wrong. Money, scale, and opportunity are not the filter. This is.

### The Core Theological Distinction — Descension, Not Ascension
Every guru, every self-help system, every spiritual school is built on the same architecture: *ascension.* You climb. You improve. You become a better version of yourself.

Christianity is the only belief built on *descension.* God came down. Jesus descended to earth, died for us, and now lives inside us through the Holy Spirit. The movement is downward — toward people, not above them.

Robert-Jan's entire life is descension in practice:
- From millionaire → campervan
- From guru with 1,200 students → student on his knees
- From 750€/hr → playing for free on a beach
- From record deal → busking in the street

He didn't preach it. He *lived* it. This is the distinction that no competitor can claim, because it is not a brand position — it is a biography.

### The Villain — "Schijnheilig"
The villain is not a person. The villain is a force: **performative holiness.**

*Schijnheilig* (Dutch) — people who found God and built a wall around Him. The church bubble that confirms itself from the inside, forgets where it came from, and expects newcomers to already know the dress code. The Christianity that demands you climb up to it instead of coming to meet you where you are. The spirituality that looks holy from the outside and has forgotten the point.

This is not anti-church. This is anti-wall. The distinction matters.

### The Movement
> *"We are proving that Jesus is in every room. Even this one."*

## PART 2 — ORIGIN STORY (The Epiphany Bridge)

*Use this arc for pitches, bios, interviews, and content. It is a true story told in the structure that creates trust.*

**Backstory:** Born in the Netherlands. At age 5, recording songs off the radio onto a cassette player. First CD: Queen Greatest Hits — played until it wore out. Made a guitar from a tennis racket with "Red Special" written on A4 paper, wanting to be both Freddie Mercury *and* Brian May. From the beginning, it wasn't about performing — it was about *being* music.

**The Road:** Built Dream or Donate, the largest Dutch/Belgian crowdfunding platform — €6M raised. Walked away from a major record deal at 21 because it meant becoming "a monkey on a stage." Became a millionaire through coaching, crypto, real estate, and investing. Attended a Tony Robbins event with $5K front-row seats — watched the manufactured emotion, the planted team members crying, the staredown across the room — and in that moment understood: all of this is ascension. People paying to be pumped up. It left him empty.

**The Wall:** The platform was hacked. He was blackmailed. He gave away all his Bitcoin to people who broke their promise. The relationship failed. The community at the villa collapsed. "There were 1,000 ways I could have handled it better. I'm very thankful for how things went, because it made me who I am today."

**The Campervan Years:** Six years. On and off Tenerife. Built a spiritual school called Senang School — 1,200+ students. Became a kind of guru. Then realised it felt fake. "It comes from your own strength." Quit the school. Started busking in the streets: 10€/hour → 100€/hour → restaurants, bars, weddings, 5-star hotels → 750€/hour for private business events. Peak earning. Still not happy.

**The Epiphany:** Got on his knees. Prayed to Jesus. Asked to be happy *like he was as a child* — before smoking, alcohol, women, status, money. Just the simple, unguarded happiness of a child. Got baptised on his favourite beach not long after. Volunteered at a local church for nearly a year, almost full time. Then left — because the institution had become more important than the people it was supposed to reach.

**The Transformation:** Now plays wherever he can. Preferably free. Not because money is evil — but because the people who might need this most are often the ones who can't afford a ticket. "If I reach 1M monthly listeners and people see the movement, they will financially support me. But that's not why I do it."

**The Inner Change:** "I feel happy as a child all day, every day now. Because I know I have a father in heaven who loves me. When I walk. When I'm drinking coffee. When I'm doing my shows. A lot. A lot."

**The Metric That Matters:** Not Spotify numbers. Not revenue. Not followers. One thing: *"You did well, my good and faithful servant."* (Matthew 25:23)

## PART 3 — THE AUDIENCE (The Seeker)

### Who They Are
Not a demographic. A condition.

**Name:** The Seeker.

Someone searching for God in their own way — the way most humans search — through drugs, sex, ecstasy, relationships, experiences, escapes. Not finding it. Trying harder. Still not finding it. And then walking into a room where something is different. A joy they didn't expect. A hope they hadn't felt before. A freedom they had never found anywhere else.

They may not call it God. They may not call it anything. But they felt it.

### External Problem (what they're visibly experiencing)
They've tried everything that's supposed to work: the substances, the scenes, the relationships, the self-help books, the retreats. None of it lasts. The escape ends. The high fades. Something is still missing and they don't have a word for it.

### Internal Problem (the emotional wound)
They feel spiritually homeless. They know they believe in *something* — but the church felt either hypocritical or irrelevant, and the rave felt true but hollow. There is no room that was made for both halves of who they are.

### Philosophical Problem (the injustice)
They were never told that sacred experience and a 140 BPM dance floor can exist in the same room. Nobody showed them this was possible. The church took one half. The club took the other. The lie is that they have to choose.

### The False Belief They're Carrying
- "I can't dance like that sober."
- "Believing in God means Sunday morning in a building I don't belong in."
- "Jesus doesn't show up at raves."
- "I'm too far gone / too messed up / too much for the church crowd."

### Before State → After State
**Before:** Searching. Escaping. Numbing. Finding connection that disappears when the night ends.

**After:** Something deeper stays. They don't have a name for it yet. But they tell their friend the next day: *"Man — you just should have been there."*

### Who This Is NOT For
People who came for the content. Filming themselves for the algorithm. Living the influencer performance instead of actually being present. They might still be touched — you never know how a seed lands — but they are not who you're speaking to.

### "People Like Them Do Things Like This" (Godin)
They go searching. They stay curious. They are suspicious of institutions but not of truth. They love music that moves something deeper than their body. They have felt God in unexpected places — a sunset, a conversation, a melody — even if they don't call it God. When they find a room where both halves of them are welcome, they come back.

## PART 4 — POSITIONING (The Third Path)

- **Christian worship music:** Sacred, institutional, church-bound. Designed for people who already believe and already know the language.
- **Regular techno/psytrance:** The escape. The forgetting. Moving your body to forget the week, forget the rules, forget yourself for a few hours.

### What Doesn't Exist (Until Now)
A room where ancient biblical truth is encoded into 145 BPM tribal psytrance and 130 BPM organic-nomadic electronic music — Bedouin rhythm, oud, handpan, Hebrew prayer mantras, sacred geometry — and people experience the sacred without needing the vocabulary.

**Not better church. Not better rave. A third thing.**

### The Positioning in One Sentence (StoryBrand format)
*For people who feel something sacred when they lose themselves on a dance floor — but have never found a place where both could exist at the same time.*

### The One-Liner (for introductions)
*"I'm a DJ and singer. Really cool music — boom-ba-die-boom — dance together, great party, in the name of Jesus. It's a really nice energy to have a party in."*

### The New Opportunity (Brunson)
Not "Christian music that's cooler." Not "techno with Bible verses." Something new: **the discovery that Jesus was already in the room before the night began.** The music is just the moment people notice.

### The Purple Cow (Godin)
In a room full of techno DJs, one who is quietly, unperformatively rooted in Jesus — who might drink a beer after his set, who prays between tracks, who thanks God for giving us bodies to dance with — is the one people remember. The cognitive dissonance *is* the remarkability. "Holy Rave" is a purple cow because it shouldn't logically work, and it does.

## PART 5 — VOICE SPECIFICATIONS

### The Voice in One Sentence
A man who has been to the bottom, found joy there, and wants to share it — without making you feel guilty for where you've been.

### The Attractive Character Archetype (Brunson)
**The Reluctant Hero meets The Adventurer.**
He didn't set out to build a movement. He followed a calling. The music became the evidence. He is not a preacher — he is a witness. There is a difference, and it matters enormously.

### The Tone Spectrum
| Attribute | IS | IS NOT |
|---|---|---|
| Sacred | Quietly, genuinely sacred | Preachy, performatively holy |
| Honest | "Nobody knows the truth until we die. But I believe." | Certain, claiming absolute authority |
| Joyful | Child-like, unguarded, free | Manufactured, hype, performed happiness |
| Raw | Direct, unpolished, real | Unfiltered in ways that distract from the message |
| Humble | "All we can do is plant seeds" | Self-deprecating, uncertain about the mission |
| Ancient | Rooted in something older than this moment | Historical, dusty, academic |

### Voice Characteristics — The Six

**1. Descending, Not Ascending**
The voice never positions RJM above the audience. It does not lecture, improve, fix, or elevate. It comes *toward* people. It meets them where they are. Every caption, email, and bio should feel like it was written from the floor, not from a stage.

**2. Specific Over Generic**
*"130 BPM ethnic tribal rhythm that makes your body do things it's never done before"* beats *"amazing music."*
*"IN THE NAME OF JESUS — people screaming and dancing like they've never danced before"* beats *"an incredible spiritual experience."*
Specificity is trust. Vagueness is noise.

**3. Plant Seeds, Don't Harvest**
The content does not pressure. It does not convert. It does not explain the theology unless asked. It places a moment, a lyric, a beat, a sentence — and trusts that God does the rest. All we can do is plant seeds.

**4. Present Tense, First Person, Alive**
Not "Robert-Jan creates music that..." — "I'm a DJ and singer. Boom-ba-die-boom. Dance together."
The voice is *in the moment.* It reports from inside the experience, not from above it. It documents, not performs.

**5. Honest About Uncertainty**
"Nobody knows the truth, until we die. But I believe." This is the most powerful sentence in the brand arsenal. It disarms cynicism instantly. It does not claim superiority. It shares conviction without crushing doubt. The voice is allowed to not have all the answers.

**6. Joy Is the Primary Emotion**
Not urgency. Not scarcity. Not hustle. Joy. Child-like, unworried, free joy. "I feel happy as a child all day, every day now." That is what the voice conveys — not "here's what you're missing" but "here is what I found and I want you to feel it too."

### Words We Use
free / alive / ancient / nomadic / tribal / together / real / present / seeds / feel / move / body / joy / sacred / raw / love / honest / found / searching / unexpected / everywhere / desert / wilderness / fire

### Words We Never Use
blessed (in the Christianese sense) / anointed / spiritual journey / elevated consciousness / manifestation / vibration / safe space / worship music / Christian music / sober rave / exclusive / ascend / better version of yourself / curated / intentional (as a performance word) / authentic (ironic — people who claim it rarely are)

### The Visualization Test (from BRAND_DNA.md)
Every sentence should produce a mental image. Ask: can the reader *see* this?

**Fails:** "A transformative spiritual experience."
**Passes:** "People screaming IN THE NAME OF JESUS at 140 BPM in a rave cave at midnight."

### The "One Mississippi" Test
The value proposition in under 2 seconds. If you have to explain it for 30 seconds before they understand — rewrite it.

### The Two-Stage Funnel
**Music builds the audience. Events convert them.**

| Stage | Content type | Target feeling | Action |
|-------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Build | Music clips, studio, track promo | "I need to save this / hear more / follow" | Search track on Spotify |
| Convert | Crowd footage, live sets, Holy Rave | "I need to be in that room" | Attend → become tribe |

Music content drives the 1M listeners north star — discovery at scale, streams, saves, follows.
Events are where curious listeners become true believers. The room does what the algorithm can't.

Both CTAs still point to Spotify for new people discovering the content cold.

### The FOMO Formula (Viral Content — from his own words)
Real footage of people dancing + contrast hook in text overlay ("Jesus at a techno rave?") + track as audio + under 40 seconds + location tag. The cognitive dissonance *is* the hook. Never explain it. Show it.

### The Prayer Moment Formula (between tracks)
Short. Spontaneous. Specific. Not a sermon.
*"Thank you God for these bodies we can dance with. For the sunshine, the ocean, the voices we can sing with. In the name of Jesus — let's go."*
Under 30 seconds. Then drop the next track.
