## ❌ SMYKM Violations — All 21 Podcast Drafts

Every single podcast pitch draft fails on the same rules. All 21 share these violations:

### 1. Word Count — FAIL (limit: 120 words)
All drafts range from **220–340 words**. Average overage: **+190 words**. None come close to the limit.

### 2. Subject Lines — FAIL (limit: 2–4 words)
All subject lines are 5–12 words and telegraphed:
- ❌ `Guest Pitch: Breakthrough Success — The Breakthrough That Came Through Breakdown` (11 words)
- ❌ `Gastpitch: De Ondernemer die Alles Kwijtraakte — en Opnieuw Begon` (10 words)
- ❌ `Guest Pitch: On Purpose with Jay Shetty — From Multimillionaire to Music and Meaning` (14 words)

None would pass the "could it apply to anyone else" test.

### 3. SMYKM Trigger — FAIL (must open with researched observation)
All openers start with a **compliment about the show**, not a specific researched observation:
- ❌ `"Hey Jay, On Purpose is one of the few shows that actually goes deep..."` — generic show compliment
- ❌ `"Hey Gerhard, Groeivoer is precies het soort gesprek dat ik zoek..."` — generic show compliment
- ❌ `"Hallo Walter, Christsein im Business ist genau der Raum, den ich gesucht habe..."` — generic show compliment

None reference a specific episode, quote, LinkedIn post, or verifiable observation about the host.

### 4. Close — FAIL (must be a low-friction question)
All drafts close with a statement, not a question:
- ❌ `"Would love to be a guest."` — statement
- ❌ `"Zou graag te gast zijn."` — statement
- ❌ `"Ich würde mich freuen, bei dir zu Gast zu sein."` — statement

### 5. Banned Phrases — PARTIAL FAIL
Several drafts contain:
- ❌ `"My name is Robert-Jan Mastenbroek"` — explicitly banned opening
- ❌ `"I've been following [Show] for a while"` — pleasantry, not a trigger
- ❌ Long paragraphs (3–5 sentences each) — violates white space rule

### 6. Faith Jargon — PARTIAL FAIL
Some drafts lean on spiritualized language ("Ich kehrte zu meinem Glauben zurück... zu Gott, nicht zur Institution") that over-announces meaning instead of letting it emerge through the story.
